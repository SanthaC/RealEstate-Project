How It Works

A great platform to buy, sell and rent your properties without any agent or commisions

  • Evaluate Property

    Begin by asking your buyer or seller for their most recent bank statement. Request all financial statements and records for the property. Determine the purchase price, contingent on your buyer or seller. Verify the market value of the property. Analyze the financial health of the buyer or seller by examining their income, expenses, and assets. Check the buyer's credit and background, as well as the seller's. Investigate the title of the property and any potential liens or encumbrances. Consider any unusual transactions or activity related to the property. Consult with an attorney or a certified property inspector for legal and structural evaluations. Always follow applicable laws and regulations during the evaluation process.

  • Meeting With Agent

    Visit your local real estate agent's office or call their phone number to schedule a meeting. Alternatively, you can attend an open house event at the property you are interested in. This is a great way to get a feel for the area and ask questions directly to the agent. You can also find local agents online through websites like Realtor.com, Zillow, and Trulia. Browse their profiles and find one that best fits your needs. Another option is to contact your friends, family, or colleagues who may know of a good real estate agent. Word of mouth is often the most effective way to find a reputable agent. Always verify the credentials and background of any agent you plan to meet with. It's essential to choose someone you can trust and feel comfortable working with

  • Close The Deal

    Assess the Property: Carefully inspect the property, from the curb to the roof, to ensure it meets your needs and preferences. Get a Home Inspection: Schedule a professional home inspection to evaluate the structural integrity, systems, and overall condition of the property. Obtain Financing: Work with a mortgage lender to secure a loan that meets your budget and credit qualifications. Negotiate the Purchase Price: Be prepared to negotiate the purchase price of the property, which may involve offering a higher initial offer or accepting lower repairs. Prepare a Purchase and Settlement Agreement: A real estate agent will typically help you draft a legally binding contract outlining the terms and conditions of the property sale. Gather Closing Documents: Before closing, gather all necessary documentation, such as a copy of your ID, proof of funds for the down payment, and a power of attorney (if applicable). Attend the Closing: In most jurisdictions, the closing, or settlement, is a legal meeting at which the property title is transferred from the seller to the buyer. Take Care of any outstanding repairs or maintenance items.
